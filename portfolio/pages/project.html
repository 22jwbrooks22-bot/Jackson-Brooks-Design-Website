Project Description

Designed for a second-year architecture studio, this 28,000-square-foot Poetry Foundation building is envisioned as a monumental Spanish Colonial landmark in downtown Riverside. Preserving the historical character of the style was a primary design objective, resulting in a composition that emphasizes symmetry, hierarchy, and permanence. The building is organized around a strong central axis, with a dominant rotunda and balanced wings that reinforce its civic presence within the urban fabric.

The exterior is defined by white plaster walls, deep arcades, clay tile roofs, and buttresses that support and articulate the mass of the structure while contributing to its traditional architectural language. The design draws inspiration from historic Spanish Colonial precedents, creating a building that feels rooted in place and history rather than following contemporary architectural trends.

Programmatically, the building is organized to separate public and private functions. The first floor contains offices, administrative spaces, and the private library collection. It also provides direct access to the performance hall, allowing event crowds to enter and exit efficiently while keeping noise and activity isolated from the upper levels. The second floor houses the primary public poetry collection, creating a dedicated environment for reading and research. The third floor contains gallery spaces and a café, offering visitors opportunities for reflection, exhibition, and social interaction while enjoying elevated views of the city. Together, these spaces create a clear organizational hierarchy that supports both functionality and architectural expression.
